General Design → Fabrication Pipeline

1
Model
Create vectors (Inkscape/Illustrator) or 3D (Fusion 360/Rhino).
2
Prepare
Attached logo to Cricket Design. Printed the logo as a sticker for Ipad .
3
Export
Export SVG/DXF for 2D or post‑process G‑code for CNC with the right post.
4
Setup
Secure stock, focus/zero tools, set origin, enable extraction and air assist.
5
Test
Run a small test: power/speed chips (laser) or surfacing/pocket test (CNC).
6
Cut
Stay with the machine; be ready to pause. Inspect edges, adjust as needed.

Tip: Document settings per material (e.g., 3mm birch: 35% power @ 15mm/s; 6mm cast acrylic: 65% @ 8mm/s). This builds a local library for consistent results.

Materials & Recommended Settings (Starting Points)

Material Tool Thickness Start Settings Notes

Cast Acrylic Laser 3–6 mm Engrave 20–30%, Cut 55–70% @ 6–12 mm/s Use air assist; avoid flame‑polished edges near joints.
Cardboard Laser Single wall Power 15–25%, 25–35 mm/s Highly flammable—never leave unattended.
