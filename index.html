Senior Design Project

From problem framing to a polished final prototype and presentation.

Problem Statement

Clearly defines the engineering challenge, stakeholders, functional requirements, constraints, and success metrics.

  • Scope: What problem, for whom, and why it matters.
  • Requirements: Performance targets & constraints (size, cost, safety, etc.).
  • Research: Prior art, market and technical review.

Preliminary Design Review (PDR)

Concept generation, trade studies, initial modeling, and risk assessment leading to a preferred design.

  • Concepts: Multiple candidate designs & tradeoffs.
  • Models: First-order analyses & simulations.
  • Plan: Milestones, BOM, test strategy.
View artifacts ↓

Critical Design Review (CDR)

Detail design with refined analyses, manufacturing drawings, and validation plan to authorize build.

  • Drawings: DFM/DFA-ready documentation.
  • Analysis: Loads, tolerances, reliability.
  • Validation: Test protocols aligned to requirements.
Dive deeper ↓

Final Presentation

Results, prototype demonstration, lessons learned, and recommendations for future work.

  • Demo: Prototype performance vs. targets.
  • Outcomes: KPIs, failures, and improvements.
  • Next: Roadmap and commercialization notes.
